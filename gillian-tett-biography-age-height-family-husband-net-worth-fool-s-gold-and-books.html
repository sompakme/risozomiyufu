Gillian Tett Biography

Gillian Tett is a British author and journalist at the Financial Times, where she is a market and finance columnist and U.S. managing editor. She was born on July 10, 1967, in the United Kingdom. Gillian has written about the financial instruments that were part of the cause of the financial crisis that started in the fourth quarter of 2007, such as CDOs, credit default swaps, SIVs, conduits, and SPVs. Gillian became renowned for her early warning that a financial crisis was looming.

Gillian Tett Education

Gillian was educated at the North London Collegiate School, an independent school for girls in Edgware, in the London Borough of Harrow in northwest London, during which time, at the age of 17, Gillian worked for a Pakistani nonprofit.

After leaving school, Gillian went up to Clare College, Cambridge, where she earned a Ph.D. in Social Anthropology based on field research in Tajikistan in the former Soviet Union. Gillian expressed frustration with academic anthropology that in her view has been committing “intellectual suicide” and decided instead to pursue a career in journalism.
