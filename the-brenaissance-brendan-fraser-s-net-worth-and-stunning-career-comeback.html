Brendan Fraser's Film Career

Brendan Fraser's illustrious film career spans over three decades, encompassing a diverse range of roles and memorable performances.

The Canadian-American actor made his film debut in 1991 with a small role in Dogfight.

His first leading role came in the 1992 comedy Encino Man, where he hilariously portrayed a thawed-out caveman navigating modern life. The film was a moderate box office success, grossing over $40 million.

It also paved the way for more prominent projects. That same year, he tackled a serious role in School Ties, alongside rising stars Matt Damon and Ben Affleck.

The mid-1990s saw Fraser appear in a mix of box office home runs and strikeouts.

He starred in films like With Honors, Airheads, and Mrs. Winterbourne, the latter of which earned a middling $10 million on a $25 million production budget. However, 1997 marked a turning point with the release of George Of The Jungle.

This live-action adaptation catapulted Fraser to global recognition, becoming his highest-grossing film at the time, with a worldwide box office return of over $174 million.

He did have moderate success with the romantic comedy Blast From The Past (1999) and the fantasy comedy Bedazzled (2000), both raking in $29 million and $90 million, respectively.

The Academy Award winner was met with global stardom after he portrayed Rick O'Connell in the fantasy adventure franchise, The Mummy.

Sequels The Mummy Returns and The Mummy: Tomb of the Dragon Emperor effectively made him a 2000s action hero and a box office draw.

Amid these successes in the action genre, Fraser also demonstrated his range in films like The Quiet American in 2002.

After a hiatus, Fraser's career resurgence came with Darren Aronofsky's The Whale in 2021, earning him critical acclaim and his first Oscar for "Best Actor," making history as the first Canadian to win the award.

Brenda Fraser's Film Salaries

Brendan Fraser's earnings from movies reflect his evolving status in Hollywood.

Though starting modestly, his income mirrored his rising fame. Early projects like The Scout saw him bank around $1.5 million.

His global breakthrough with The Mummy earned him approximately $4 million in 1999, a figure that more than doubled to $10 million for Bedazzled the next year and further increased to $12.5 million for The Mummy Returns.

Fraser's income peaked with The Mummy: Tomb Of The Dragon Emperor in 2008, earning $14 million. However, as the years progressed, Fraser's earnings began to decline, mainly due to his hiatus and losing his leading man status.

Fraser's income declined significantly, a circumstance he attributed to having been "blacklisted" by Hollywood, following his allegations of sexual abuse against former Hollywood Foreign Press Association (HFPA) president Philip Berk.

In 2010, he received $10 million for Furry Vengeance and reportedly took a significant pay cut to $1 million for Extraordinary Measures in 2010.

Following his return to Hollywood, Fraser's status and bankability is on the rise, with recent reports indicating a resurgence in his earnings.

His recent role in The Whale reportedly earned him $12 million, highlighting a renewed demand for his talent, and underscoring the financial promise of his career comeback.

Fraser's Legal Troubles

Fraser's personal life has not been without financial setbacks.

His 2009 divorce from actress Afton Smith brought about a hefty alimony settlement. As part of their divorce, Brendan agreed to a settlement of nearly $8.7 million, almost half his estimated net worth at the time.

Additionally, he committed to paying Afton $75,000 monthly, covering both alimony and child support, totaling $900,000 annually, requiring him to earn around $2 million yearly pre-tax.

The obligation was split into a monthly alimony of $50,000 to his ex-wife for ten years, and an additional $25,000 monthly for child support. In 2011, Fraser faced a financial crisis.
