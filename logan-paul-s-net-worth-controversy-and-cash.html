Logan Paul's Biggest Controversy

However, Paul then landed something else – himself, in the crosshairs of brewing international controversy, one that would stall his fast-moving career in its tracks and dominate headlines the world over.

On December 31st 2017, Logan Paul visited the Aokigahara forest in Japan – a darkly famous location, known as a suicide site. Paul impulsively filmed the body of a suicide victim, and proceeded to behave in a manner widely deemed as disrespectful and insensitive.

Fallout from the controversy was swift and strict: YouTube ended the monetization of his videos. He was also fired from the series Foursome.

2018 was a difficult year for Logan Paul, calling into question the future of his next movie, The Thinning: New World Order. Paul apologized for his behavior, and after a few months, he began reintegrating his presence on platforms like YouTube.

But Logan Paul had to restart his acting career, and his self-written Airplane Mode flopped spectacularly, earning an IMDb rating of just 1.8.
