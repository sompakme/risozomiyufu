The Story Behind MinkDog: Chef Tyler Lewis’s Long Term Love Affair With Food

For Chef Tyler Lewis, MinkDog is the culmination of a lifelong relationship with food. He grew up in a close-knit Italian-American family that valued being immersed in nature and gathering around the table for meals.

During his childhood, Chef Tyler remembers foraging for edible mushrooms and bonding with the natural world. His family taught him that lovingly crafted dishes are more than the sum of their parts—they’re what brings us together.

That love of home cooking sunk deep into his psyche. While getting his undergraduate degree in environmental science and eco-design, he picked up shifts working for local restaurants.

It was always a side job. Until it wasn’t.

After school and a few years spent denying what was plain to see, Chef Tyler embraced a career in food, becoming the head chef at a restaurant in Burlington, Vermont. Two things became clear very quickly:

First, he adored making food for people. Curating menus and working with distributors up and down the East Coast became Chef Tyler’s passion.

Second, the restaurant industry wasn’t his dream. Long hours and the inherent stress of running a kitchen kept Chef Tyler too busy to spend time creating the cuisine he loved.

So he sailed for sunnier shores, choosing instead to become a personal chef for clients from the Hamptons to the Caribbean. In many ways, this role brought him full circle. He was back working in home kitchens. Back preparing meals for small, intimate settings. And back searching for the highest quality ingredients around when possible, plucked directly from the natural world.

That brings us to the distinct and taste-bud-pleasuring products that are a part of MinkDog’s first line.

Fennel Pepper: A Spicy, Aromatic Secret Weapon

Fennel Pepper is the coveted seasoning that improves everything it’s in. If you’ve ever bitten into a dish made with fennel, you know the intensity of the flavor. Lively and mouth-watering, fennel is an excellent addition to soups and rice dishes.

Chef Tyler recommends using it on chicken, rubbing the seasoning blend onto a dry-patted bird. Truss your chicken (because you’re a real chef now) and allow it to rest before roasting it for a little over an hour. Give it some time to cool down and retain its moisture after pulling it out of the oven and take a second to congratulate yourself on cooking something that smells that good. Carve up and enjoy.

Add some zing to your dish with Fennel Pepper.

Hampton Boil: A Bite Straight Out of New England

Inspired by Chef Tyler’s years in the seafood haven of the Hamptons, this blend packs a citrusy and smoky heat. Lemon, garlic, and the addition of aromatic cardamom make this an inspired bespoke blend, perfect for clam bakes and summer seafood.

For the Hampton Boil, Chef Tyler has prepared a recipe for crab cakes. He says the key to a perfect crab cake comes down to the patties themselves.

For those, you’re going to need a lint-free cloth. Squeeze the excess moisture from his signature crab mixture—crab, a variety of sauteed vegetables, Hampton Boil blend, and binding agents you’ll have in your fridge already. Pay special attention to how you fry them and bake for an additional thirty minutes to achieve the perfect texture.

Whether you’re serving them as an appetizer or just gobbling them up for dinner, the flavor profile is sure to stun.

Bring summer to the table with the Hampton Boil spice blend.

It’s a Saturday morning. You’re having guests over tonight and have decided to make the most delicious, melt-in-your-mouth ribs possible. You’re hungry, but most of all, you’re craving something sweet, savory, and signature.

Enter, Maple Java. Packed with organic maple sugar, sourced locally from small farms in Vermont, and paired with chili peppers, Maple Java offers a robust taste.

This blend is perfect for rubbing onto anything you want to grill, but Chef Tyler has offered a recipe for oven-roasted pork shoulder that will elevate your taco game. Time is the key component here, with the pork and a bed of vegetables spending at least four and a half hours in the oven. The smell in your kitchen will be nothing short of divine all day.

Spice up any meal with smoky Maple Java.
