Who Owns Happy Dad Hard Seltzer? Happy Dad Hard Seltzer Company History

Publish date: 2024-08-14

Happy Dad Hard Seltzer is a popular beverage brand known for its easy-to-drink hard seltzer with refreshing flavors. But who owns this growing brand, and what is its company background? Let’s take a closer look.

Founded in June 2021, Happy Dad Hard Seltzer was created by Sam Shahidi, John Shahidi, and Kyle Forgeard. These founders, who are also known for their involvement in the Full Send Podcast, have successfully built a brand that resonates with their audience.

Operating out of Orange County, California, Happy Dad has quickly gained popularity, amassing over 1.5 million followers on social media. The brand’s straightforward approach, offering a regular 12 oz can with simple, refreshing flavors, has struck a chord with consumers.

Despite being a relatively young brand, Happy Dad has achieved remarkable success in a short span of time. It has become one of the top 5 seltzer brands in sales at Total Wine & More. The founders’ previous experiences and connections in the liquor industry have undoubtedly contributed to the brand’s growth.

Happy Dad sets itself apart by offering an easy-to-drink, low sugar, and low carb beverage that is gluten-free. The founders believed in creating a product that they genuinely enjoyed themselves, and it has resonated with consumers.

As the brand continues to expand its presence, Happy Dad has also formed strategic partnerships with influential figures such as Elon Musk, Post Malone, and Snoop Dogg. These collaborations have further elevated the brand’s reach and attracted a wider audience.

Looking ahead, Happy Dad has ambitious goals to solidify its position as one of the top hard seltzer brands in the United States. With its unique flavors, compelling branding, and dedicated founders, the future looks bright for Happy Dad Hard Seltzer.

The Founders’ Journey and Vision

The story behind Happy Dad Hard Seltzer is a testament to the entrepreneurial spirit and vision of its founders. Sam Shahidi, John Shahidi, and Kyle Forgeard, known for their involvement in the popular Full Send Podcast, embarked on a new adventure to create their own beverage brand.

The journey began with their success and influence in the digital realm, where they discovered the power of their online presence. Inspired by their online following, they saw an opportunity to venture into the beverage industry and create a brand that resonated with their audience. And so, Happy Dad Hard Seltzer was born.

The founders’ vision was clear – to convert beer drinkers to hard seltzer while offering a product that appealed to both male and female consumers. They wanted to fill a gap in the market and provide a refreshing alternative that would cater to a diverse range of tastes.

With their previous experiences and connections in the liquor industry, Sam, John, and Kyle were able to efficiently develop and market Happy Dad Hard Seltzer. Their understanding of consumer preferences and emerging trends enabled them to create a product that stood out in a saturated market.

Through their entrepreneurial spirit, vision, and dedication, the founders transformed their online influence into a successful beverage brand. Today, Happy Dad Hard Seltzer continues to grow and thrive, delivering a drink that brings joy and happiness to consumers across the country.

Product Offering and Features

Happy Dad Hard Seltzer offers a 12-can variety pack that includes four flavors: Wild Cherry, Lemon-Lime, Pineapple, and Watermelon. Each 12 oz can has an ABV of 5% and is gluten-free with only 1g of sugar and 100 calories. The beverage also includes electrolytes and contains no artificial flavors. The product is designed to provide a refreshing and enjoyable drinking experience.

Happy Dad Hard Seltzer’s variety pack ensures that you have a taste for every mood. With flavors like Wild Cherry, Lemon-Lime, Pineapple, and Watermelon, there’s something to satisfy every palate. Whether you’re looking for a classic fruit flavor or a more unique twist, Happy Dad has you covered.
